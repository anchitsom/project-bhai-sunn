# Bhai Sunn: A Hindi-First Open-Source Alternative to Alexa

**Status:** vision document, v0.1, 2026-04-29

## What this is

Bhai Sunn is a household voice assistant for Hindi-speaking homes. It runs on hardware you own. It does not phone home. It treats Hindi as the design language, not as a translation overlay on a product built for English. The name is a pun: *bhai* (brother) and *sunn* (listen) — the register a household actually uses, not the imported corporate vocative of "Alexa".

It is built for the 600 million Hindi speakers whom Alexa, Google Home, and Siri serve as an afterthought, and for any household that does not want its kitchen, bedroom, and bathroom audio routed to a Seattle, Mountain View, or Cupertino data centre.

## The problem with Alexa in an Indian home

Three failures, layered:

1. **Hindi quality is poor.** Alexa Hindi is a UI veneer over an English-first product. Speech recognition fumbles on Hinglish code-switching, which is the default conversational mode in urban Indian households. Responses are translation-flavoured. Regional pronunciation is treated as deviation from a North-Indian standard.

2. **Account model assumes one user.** Indian households are joint, multi-generational, and shared. Alexa's account-and-skill model assumes a single primary user with a credit card and a Prime subscription. A grandmother asking for a recipe should not be a sub-profile of her grandson's Amazon login.

3. **Data leaves the home.** Every utterance is recorded, transcribed, and stored in Amazon's infrastructure. The household's most intimate conversation surface becomes training data and ad-targeting signal for a foreign company under a foreign legal regime. The "convenience tax" is paid in surveillance.

The wiki frames this clearly. **Platform lock-in** is *"a sinister form of rent-extraction, functioning like a demagogue that extracts value from both the consumer and the seller. The only way to defeat platform lock-in is through interoperability and the adoption of local-ai, which keeps data ownership with the individual rather than the provider."* Alexa is the textbook case: proprietary skills, integrated ecosystem (Echo plus Ring plus Music plus Prime), and data gravity (years of household usage logs that cannot be exported).

**Vendor lock-in** in the AI era operates through three mechanisms, all of which Alexa exemplifies:
- proprietary APIs (the Skills SDK)
- data gravity (the household profile)
- integrated ecosystems (the Echo plus everything else)

The wiki's prescription for breaking lock-in: standardised protocols, local inference, cross-platform clients. Bhai Sunn implements all three.

## Why now

Four things have arrived at the same time:

1. **Open small LLMs are good enough at Hindi.** Gemma 2 (2B and 9B) and Llama 3.x are multilingual by training, not by translation. They follow Hindi instructions and respond in idiomatic Hindi or Hinglish without a translation layer.

2. **Whisper large-v3-turbo runs fast on Apple Silicon.** mlx-whisper turns a Mac Studio M1 Max into a high-quality, low-latency Hindi STT engine. The same workload on a Pi takes ten times longer.

3. **Wyoming protocol decouples mic from brain.** Home Assistant's voice protocol is open, audio-streaming, and modular. Any device that speaks Wyoming can be a satellite. Any service that speaks Wyoming can be a brain. The satellite and the brain do not have to be from the same vendor, on the same architecture, or even running the same OS.

4. **Custom wake-words are trainable on a laptop.** OpenHomeFoundation's micro-wake-word library lets a household train a wake phrase from a few hours of recordings (or synthetic samples generated by Piper). Bhai Sunn becomes its own keyword, owned by the household, not licensed from a vendor.

These four shifts compose. Without all four, the architecture does not work. With them, the architecture is straightforward.

## The wedge

Hindi-first is not a feature. It is the design centre.

- **Hinglish is the conversational default**, not an edge case. Code-switching mid-sentence is normal speech. The pipeline is tuned for it from the wake-word onwards.
- **Family-shared, not account-bound.** No login. No primary user. The household, not the customer, is the unit.
- **LAN-first.** The satellite reaches the brain over the home network. Internet is optional, and only used for explicit web-lookup intents. A power cut at the ISP does not turn the assistant into a paperweight.
- **Configurable from a household policy file.** What the assistant should know about the home, who can do what, the names of the children and their bedtimes — these are written in plain Markdown that the household owns and edits.

## The political layer

Pope writes in *Platformland*: *"if code is law, then software—the manifestation of code in the world—is politics."* A voice assistant in 200M+ Indian households is political infrastructure. It shapes what gets asked, what gets answered, what gets recorded, and what gets monetised. That infrastructure should be public-interest software, not a foreign-owned funnel.

Bhai Sunn is built in that frame. It is not anti-commercial — it is anti-capture. The household keeps its data, can swap any part of the stack, and can fork the project for Marathi, Bhojpuri, Tamil, Bengali, or any language Whisper and the available open LLMs cover. A village Anganwadi worker should be able to ask the assistant in Hindi about a vaccination schedule without an Amazon account.

This is sovereign-AI-industrial-policy applied at household scale.

## Non-goals (v1)

- **Not a smart home controller.** Home Assistant does that. Bhai Sunn integrates with HA via Wyoming as a v2 step; it does not reinvent it.
- **Not a music streamer.** Music Assistant does that. Same v2 integration path.
- **Not a frontier general assistant.** v1 matches Alexa's actual usage envelope — timers, reminders, weather, lists, news, factoid lookup, light switch — not GPT-4 reasoning. Bigger ambitions can come once the pipeline is solid.

## Success criteria for v1

- Wake on "Bhai Sunn" with under 2% false-positive rate in a noisy kitchen.
- Hindi command-to-response latency under 3 seconds end-to-end on the Mac Studio brain.
- Hindi STT accuracy at parity with or above Alexa Hindi (Whisper large-v3-turbo benchmarks suggest above is the realistic target).
- Zero cloud dependency for any core path. Internet may be used for explicit web-lookup, never for STT, LLM, TTS, or wake.
- One Pi 5 satellite plus one Mac Studio brain runs the whole stack. Adding a second satellite is a config-file edit, not a re-pairing.

## What v1 enables

- **Forking.** Train the wake-word on a Marathi phrase. Swap the Whisper language code. Pick a Marathi Piper voice. Same architecture, new language, in an afternoon.
- **Public-interest deployments.** A village clinic, a school, a panchayat office can deploy this without commercial onboarding. The brain can run on a refurbished workstation; satellites can be ESP32-cost devices.
- **Vendor-neutral household intelligence.** Replace the Mac Studio with any box that runs the brain services. Replace any satellite with any Wyoming-speaking device. Nothing in the household is pinned to a single vendor.

## What this is not, again

This is not a clone of Alexa with the Amazon logo filed off. The product surface is shaped by a different premise: that household conversation is private, that Hindi is primary not secondary, and that the household, not a foreign company, is the unit of design. The architecture follows from the premise, not from a feature parity table.
